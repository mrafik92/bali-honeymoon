Bangkok

Days 1–3 · 3 nights · Temples, river, neon & night-market romance

🚚 Getting There

  • Airport to Riverside: Airport Rail Link + taxi $8–12 | private transfer $25–35
  • Travel time: 45–60 min from BKK (Suvarnabhumi)
  • Getting around: BTS Skytrain $1–2 | MRT $1–2 | Grab $2–6
  • River boats: Chao Phraya Express $0.50 | Hotel shuttles often free
  • To Chiang Mai: 1h flight $45–75 (AirAsia / Thai Smile / Nok)

📷 What to Visit in Bangkok

Wat Arun at Sunset

The Temple of Dawn glowing rose-gold across the Chao Phraya as the sky melts into violet — one of Asia's most photographed sunsets.
Cross the river on the þ3 ferry from Tha Tien pier. Climb the prang for the view.
$3 entry

Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew

A 218,400 m² complex of golden spires — the spiritual heart of Thailand and home to the sacred Emerald Buddha.
Open 8:30am–3:30pm. Dress modestly — covered shoulders & knees.
$14 entry

Wat Pho & Reclining Buddha

A 46-metre gilded reclining Buddha — serene, immense, and quietly humbling.
Combo with the on-site traditional Thai massage school — $8 for 30 min.
$6 entry

Chao Phraya Dinner Cruise

Sliding past illuminated Wat Arun, the Grand Palace, and Rama VIII Bridge on a candlelit dinner cruise — pure honeymoon.
Saffron Cruise & Manohra are the upscale picks — 2 hours.
$45–75/person

Yaowarat Chinatown Food Walk

Neon-lit Yaowarat Road after dark, vendors firing woks on the sidewalk, the air thick with garlic, chili, and grilled prawns.
Hit Nai Ek for guay jub, T&K Seafood for grilled prawns, Pa Tong Go Savoey for dessert.
Meals $3–10

Mahanakhon SkyWalk at Dusk

Step onto a glass floor 314m above Bangkok as the city dissolves into a sea of lights — not for the faint-hearted.
Go up at 5:30pm to catch sunset AND city lights in one ticket.
$22/person

Lumpini Park Swan Boats

A green oasis in the middle of the megacity — pedal a paddle-boat on the lake, watch monitor lizards sun themselves on the banks.
Pedal-boats $2 / 30 min. Best at 7am or after 4pm.
$2 boat

Couples Thai Massage at Health Land

Side-by-side 2-hour Thai massage & aroma therapy in a candlelit suite — a soft landing after temple-hopping all day.
Health Land & Asia Herb Association are reliable picks. Book ahead.
$35–55/couple
"Bangkok reveals its most romantic side in the spaces between its contrasts — flower markets at first light, temple courtyards steeped in quiet, sunset rooftops above the skyline, and candlelit moments by the river."— Banyan Tree Bangkok (translated from local guidance)

Chiang Mai

Days 4–7 · 4 nights · Lanna temples, jungle & slow mountain mornings

🚚 Getting There

  • Bangkok → Chiang Mai: 1h flight $45–75/person (AirAsia / Thai Smile / Nok)
  • CNX airport to Old City: Grab $4–6 | 15 min
  • Getting around: Walkable Old City | Songthaew (red truck) $1–2
  • Scooter rental: $5–7/day with deposit
  • To Krabi: 2h flight to KBV $60–110 (no direct — via BKK or DMK)

📷 What to Visit in Chiang Mai

Wat Phra That Doi Suthep at Sunset

Climb the 309-step naga staircase as monks chant for evening prayer — below, Chiang Mai glitters across the valley at sunset.
Songthaew from Old City $4 round-trip. Best at 5pm.
$2 entry

Elephant Nature Park (Ethical Sanctuary)

Feed and bathe rescued elephants in their forest home — no riding, no shows, just sharing space with these gentle giants. Many couples rate it the trip highlight.
Book Elephant Nature Park or ChangChill 2+ weeks ahead. Half-day or full-day.
$80–160/person

Thai Cooking Class (Market + Class)

Start at a fresh market choosing kaffir lime leaves & galangal, then cook 5 dishes together in an open-air kitchen — couples' favorite.
Asia Scenic, Thai Farm Cooking, & Smart Cook are top-rated.
$30–45/person

Old City Temple Cycling Tour

Bicycle the quiet sois of the Old City, hopping from Wat Phra Singh's golden chedi to Wat Chedi Luang's 600-year-old ruined pagoda.
Bike rentals $3/day. Most temples open 6am–6pm, free or small donation.
Free / donation

Doi Inthanon National Park Day Trip

Thailand's highest mountain — cloud forest hikes, twin royal pagodas, waterfalls, and crisp 18°C air at the summit.
Day tour with driver: $35–50/person. Stop at Wachirathan Waterfall.
$5 park entry

Sunday Walking Street + Night Bazaar

Lanterns glowing over Tha Phae Road, artisans selling silver and handwoven scarves, stalls of grilled satay and sticky rice in bamboo.
Sunday is the big one — 4pm to midnight. Eat your way through.
Free (eats $1–3)

Couples Lanna Spa (Fah Lanna)

Hidden garden spa just outside the moat — 2-hour Lanna couples package with herbal compress, hot stone, and floral foot ritual.
Fah Lanna or Oasis Spa — book a couple's package for 2.5–3h.
$60–90/couple
"Recommend this place — the khao soi broth simmers for 4 hours and the chili paste is pounded fresh every day. There's nothing else like it."— Khao Soi Nimman via &แม่มณีชวนชิม (translated from Thai)

Krabi & Railay

Days 8–14 · 7 nights · Limestone cliffs, longtail boats & Andaman sunsets

🚚 Getting There

  • Chiang Mai → Krabi: Connecting flight via BKK $60–110/person (3h total)
  • Airport to Phulay Bay/Nong Thale: Private transfer $30–45 | 35 min
  • To Railay: Longtail from Ao Nang $3–5/person | 15 min by boat (no road)
  • Getting around: Hotel transfer + day tours | Scooter $7–10/day
  • Krabi → home: Direct to BKK $50–90 or Phuket transfer $30 + flight

📷 What to Visit in Krabi & Railay

Railay Beach & Phra Nang Cave

Step off the longtail onto a cinema-set beach — soft white sand framed by 300m vertical limestone walls, climbers above, kayaks gliding by.
Longtail from Ao Nang $4 each way. Walk between West and East Railay and Phra Nang.
$8 boat round-trip

Four Islands Longtail Tour

Chicken Island, Tup Island, Mor Island and Poda — snorkel reefs, walk the famous sandbar at low tide, eat lunch under a palm.
Half or full day. Bring sunscreen, water, dry bag.
$22–30/person

Hong Islands Kayak & Lagoon

Paddle into the Hong lagoon — a sea-flooded amphitheatre walled by 100m cliffs, water so still and clear you can see fish 5m down.
Speedboat + kayak combo $40–55. Go early to beat day-trippers.
$40–55/person

Tiger Cave Temple (Wat Tham Sua) Sunrise

1,237 steep steps climb a limestone outcrop to a golden Buddha — a 360° sunrise over the Krabi jungle and karst islands waits at the top.
Start by 5am to summit at dawn. Heavy on the legs — bring water.
Free (donation)

Ao Thalane Mangrove Kayak

Glide between towering mangroves and through hidden sea caves on flat water — cool, shaded, with monkeys and kingfishers overhead.
Half-day guided tour, calm water, suitable for beginners.
$25–35/person

Phi Phi Islands Speedboat Day

Maya Bay's perfect crescent of sand, monkey beach, Pileh Lagoon's neon water — the most iconic island day in southern Thailand.
Speedboat day trip with snorkel + lunch. Get the early departure.
$50–75/person

Ao Nang Beach Sunset Stroll

The strip lights flicker on, longtails return with the day's last tourists, the sky turns orange over the karst islands — toes in the sand with a Chang.
Free. Best beach bars on the south end.
Free

Emerald Pool & Hot Springs

Crystal-clear spring-fed pool deep in the Khao Pra-Bang Khram rainforest, then nearby cascading natural hot springs.
Day trip from Krabi — bring swimsuit, water shoes. 2h drive each way.
$6 entry

Couples Beachfront Spa at Phulay Bay

Side-by-side ESPA ritual in an overwater spa pavilion, ocean breeze, then dinner in the Sala on stilts — the honeymoon postcard.
Phulay Bay or Rayavadee spa — book a 2h package.
$140–220/couple
"Roads are rough — hire a private driver instead of a scooter. Stay at Phulay Bay or Rayavadee for the honeymoon, but a Beach Picnic at Tubkaak Resort with sunset is one of the most romantic things in Krabi."— Kant Thai travel review (translated from Thai)
