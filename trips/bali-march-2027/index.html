Ubud

Days 1–6 · 5 nights · The cultural heart of Bali

🚚 Getting There

  • Airport to Ubud: Grab/Gojek $13–19 | or private transfer $25–30
  • Travel time: 1.5–2 hours (38 km)
  • Budget tip: Kura-Kura shuttle bus from Kuta $6/person (takes 3–4h)
  • Getting around: Scooter rental $3–5/day | Gojek rides $1–3

📷 What to Visit in Ubud

Tegalalang Rice Terraces

Like walking through a living painting — layer after layer of luminous green stretching into the valley.
Go before 8am when the light is golden and the crowds are gone.
Free (small donation)

Campuhan Ridge Walk

A peaceful ridge path suspended between two river valleys — just you, the breeze, and the sound of birds at dawn.
Best at 6am — the light filters through morning mist.
Free

Sacred Monkey Forest

Ancient temple grounds where over 1,200 long-tailed macaques roam freely through the jungle — equal parts magical and mischievous.
Keep your belongings close — the monkeys are clever!
$5 entry

Tirta Empul Water Temple

A sacred spring where locals and visitors alike walk through cleansing fountains — a deeply moving Balinese ritual.
Dress respectfully — sarongs are provided.
$5 entry

Tibumana Waterfall (hidden gem)

A peaceful curtain-style waterfall tucked deep in the Bangli jungle — less crowded than Tegenungan, with butterflies and vines on the trail in.
30 min drive from Ubud — bring water shoes for the cool natural pool.
$1.30 entry

Balinese Cooking Class (Paon Bali)

Starting at the market buying fresh spices, then cooking together in an open-air kitchen surrounded by rice fields — a couples highlight.
Paon Bali is top-rated — includes market tour & lunch.
$35–50/person

Ubud Palace & Kecak Dance

The ornate palace in the heart of town hosts a candlelit Kecak dance every evening — chanting, fire, and gamelan, all under starlight.
Palace is free — Kecak dance performances at 7:30pm.
Free · Dance $7

Couples Spa Treatment

A candlelit room with the scent of frangipani — both of you side by side, melting into a 90-minute Balinese massage.
Ubud Traditional Massage is $8–12 — hotel spas are $50+.
$8–12 / 90 min
“Hidden behind narrow alleys near Ubud Palace, you'll find Sweet Orange Warung — sitting with a plate of nasi goreng watching the rice fields, you feel completely far from the tourist crowds, just the breeze and the sound of frogs.”— wisataubudbali.com (translated from Bahasa Indonesia)

Nusa Penida

Days 7–9 · 3 nights · Wild cliffs & turquoise waters

🚚 Getting There

  • Ubud → Sanur Port: Grab/Gojek $11–15 (1h)
  • Sanur → Nusa Penida: Fast boat $9–15/person | 30–45 min
  • Penida → Seminyak: Combined ferry+bus $12–17/person | ~2.5h
  • On island: Private driver $30–45/day recommended — roads are rough

📷 What to Visit in Nusa Penida

Kelingking Beach (T-Rex Cliff)

A cliff perfectly shaped like a T-Rex plunging into turquoise water — the most iconic view in all of Bali.
Arrive by 8am before tour boats. Treat as a viewpoint — the hike down is steep and the surf below is dangerous.
$0.70 entry

Angel's Billabong & Broken Beach

A natural infinity pool carved into the cliff — at low tide it's a crystal-clear jacuzzi overlooking the ocean.
Two spots are 5 min apart — check tides before going (only swimmable at low tide).
Free

Crystal Bay Snorkel & Sunset

Calm turquoise water with 300+ coral species just offshore — snorkel straight from the beach, then stay for the west-side sunset.
The afternoon spot to relax after the morning cliff hikes.
Free

Diamond Beach & Atuh Beach

White sand framed by jagged diamond-shaped cliffs — quieter than the west side, pure tropical paradise.
East coast — combine with Rumah Pohon treehouse. Long drive (50 min).
$2 entry

Snorkeling with Manta Rays

Gliding silently above a manta ray the size of a car as it sweeps past beneath you — one of those moments where time stops.
Manta Point tour — 2h, year-round but best March–October.
$13–22/person

Teletubbies Hill & Rumah Pohon

Rolling green hills that look like they belong in a fantasy film, with a wooden treehouse perched on the clifftop edge.
Rumah Pohon offers the "Thousand Islands" viewpoint.
Free
“Many travelers arrive in Nusa Penida with one goal — to see Kelingking Beach — but the island's magic is much wider. Don't try to cram every spot into one day; the roads are slow and the views deserve time.”— nuzanthra.biz.id (translated from Bahasa Indonesia)

📷 What to Visit in Seminyak

Sunset at Potato Head / KU DE TA

Lying on a daybed by the infinity pool as the sky turns orange over the ocean — cocktails in hand, your first sunset.
Potato Head is iconic — KU DE TA is more intimate. Cover charge 250k IDR redeemable on food/drinks.
Drinks from $8

Seminyak Beach & La Plancha

Miles of golden sand stretching into the sunset — grab a colorful beanbag, order a Bintang, and just watch the waves roll in.
La Plancha has the rainbow beanbags — arrive 1h before sunset.
Free

Eat Street (Jalan Kayu Aya)

A lively street lined with candlelit restaurants, cocktail bars, and boutique stores — every night feels like date night.
From local warungs to fine dining — something for every mood.
Meals $3–30

Revolver Espresso / Sisterfields brunch

The best brunch in Bali — smoothie bowls topped with fresh fruit, avocado toast, and flat whites that rival Melbourne.
Revolver has a speakeasy vibe — Sisterfields is bright and airy.
$5–10/person

Couples Spa Day

Side by side on a candlelit treatment table, frangipani-scented oil — this is the kind of spa day you'll both remember.
Prana Spa or Bodyworks — book a 2h couple's package.
$40–60 / couple

Uluwatu Sunset Kecak

An ancient cliff temple at the southern tip of Bali, hosting the most dramatic Kecak fire dance as the sun sets behind dancers and ocean.
Show starts at 6pm — arrive by 5:15 for good seats.
$8 entry
“Who said Seminyak is always expensive? Hidden among the high-end glamour, Warung Marinda lets you eat a generous portion of nasi kuning or nasi ayam lalapan for just 10,000 rupiah — sambal nampol, kindness included.”— baliinyourhands.com (translated from Bahasa Indonesia)
